What today?

While practicing my skills as a designer in web design, I decided to take on additional activities, including branding. Below are the results of work on the website of a new brand - Underground. Enjoy!

About Project

Company:

Underground. A company that manufactures and sells sportswear for discerning athletes. Products are sold in the highest quality of all sports products.

My task:

Create a landing page that will generate leads for the online shop.

Create Branding that meets customer requirements.

Prepare Handoff for developer for desktop and mobile screens.

Brand Guidelines

For the needs of the project, I created a short Brandbook to create the image of the brand's visual identification, base the website design on it, and make future projects consistent with the brand. To create a palette of 4 colors, I used the Coolors tool, which has a huge database of colors for projects. As the primary typeface, I used Raleway, which in combination with Lato typeface creates a great complement.

Style Guide

Creating a brandbook made it easier to create a style guide for the project. I initiated color shades in the range of 50-900 (Material design) to enrich the palette with more possibilities. To obtain a typographic scale I used Type Scale, based on 16 px body text I scaled the font to 1.250 - Major Third. In this way, I obtained font sizes that fully cover the needs of the project.

High-Fidelity UI Design

Once I had drafted the initial content based on the brief - Lo-Fi Wireframes, I proceeded to design the high-fidelity interface. I divided the project into individual sections, which made it easier for me to design the entire website.

Responsive views

I couldn't forget about the responsiveness of the interface, because as we know, many studies show that over 50% of website visits come from smartphones.

Takeaways

A brief description of what I was able to create.

I created the branding based on the client's expectations and his preferences for the appearance of the brand.

I built a Landing page, which is designed to generate traffic to the shop, familiarize the user with the brand and propose a subscription to the newsletter, thanks to which he will receive news in the world of sports.

When designing, I took into account responsiveness and programming capabilities so that the project could be fully programmable.

Project Summary

During the project, I did a short desk research based on client brief, created a full visual identity of the brand, created a style guide based on the Brandbook combined it with a fully responsive Landing Page. Creating something new and bringing the project to the end was a really interesting experience. During this time I learned a lot of things, which above all gave me the opportunity to solve new, real problem. All aspects that led to the finished interface is a great experience that builds my design thinking skills. Thank you for your time!
